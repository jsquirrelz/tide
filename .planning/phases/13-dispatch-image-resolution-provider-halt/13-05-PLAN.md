Close the two code-defect blockers from 13-VERIFICATION.md:

1. **CR-01 (DISPATCH-01):** milestone_controller.go reconcilePlannerDispatch dereferences a
   nil-tolerant `project` at :370 (`project.Spec.ProviderSecretRef`) and :394
   (`string(project.UID)`) without a guard. Milestone is the only unguarded planner level —
   phase guards both sites, plan refuses dispatch entirely (cascade-7).
2. **WR-03 (HALT-01):** BillingHalt recovery is silently undone by stragglers. The credproxy
   latch is process-lifetime and its synthetic 400 body deliberately contains "credit balance";
   a still-running session that latched pre-resume exits non-zero AFTER `tide resume`, and the
   envelope backstop re-stamps BillingHalt on the now-funded project.

**Chosen WR-03 semantics (option b from the work order, deliberately):** after `tide resume`,
only a billing failure from a Job created AFTER the resume may initiate a halt. `tide resume`
stamps a `tideproject.k8s/billing-resumed-at` timestamp annotation when it clears BillingHalt;
`setBillingHaltIfNeeded` skips stamping when the failing Job's creation time predates that
timestamp. Rationale over options (a)/(c): the anthropic harness builds `EnvelopeOut.Reason`
from the HEAD of stderr (`truncate(stderrBuf.String(), 256)`, subagent.go:307,342-346 keeps
`s[:n]`), and every latched pod saw a REAL upstream 400 first — so the REAL "credit balance"
text survives in a straggler's Reason no matter how the synthetic body is worded. Body rewording
alone (options a/c) cannot stop the re-stamp; only the time fence can. The synthetic body is
ALSO reworded as defense-in-depth (it is the sole manufactured-evidence channel for a subagent
container that restarts inside an already-latched pod, where the first stderr error is synthetic).
