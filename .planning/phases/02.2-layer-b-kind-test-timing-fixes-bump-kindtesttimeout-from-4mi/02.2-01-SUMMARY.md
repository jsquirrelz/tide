## Decisions Made

- **kindTestTimeout = 7 minutes (not 6).** 7m gives 2m margin above helm's 5m budget vs 6m's 60s margin. Slow CI runner variance can eat 60s but not 120s. Extra minute costs nothing — the inner test wall is bounded by helm's 5m anyway; margin is only consumed on slow paths.
- **Bundle all four source-shape edits in one atomic suite_test.go commit (Task 1).** They share the same verification surface; splitting them would quadruple the runtime verification cost without isolating risk.
- **Drop CI's outer DUR > 300 check (not split into two CI steps).** The Makefile's inner `timeout 300s` already enforces the contract on the right span (go test invocation, NOT the full make chain including unbounded prep). Re-implementing in CI just creates confusion when one fires and the other doesn't. CI's `timeout-minutes: 6` job-level fence is the genuine outer ceiling.
- **cleanupKindCluster() uses plain `exec.Command`, NOT `exec.CommandContext(ctx, ...)`.** The outer ctx is cancelled at AfterSuite's top; passing it would immediately abort cleanup. (Phase 02.2 RESEARCH §"Pitfall 7".)
- **cleanupKindCluster() filters by label `io.x-k8s.kind.cluster=tide-test` (not by container name).** Label is kind's documented internal convention and survives a future multi-node cluster.yaml change.
- **`gate_decision: BLOCKED` (NOT APPROVED) on the runtime gate** — but for a NEW, PRE-EXISTING, OUT-OF-SCOPE blocker (PVC RWX/RWO mismatch with kind's local-path provisioner). The Phase 02.1 four BLOCKED root causes are demonstrably closed at the source-shape layer; the new blocker is in the chart's PVC accessModes contract, not in the test infrastructure. Per the wave-failure-semantics contract this routes to a new phase, not to in-place iteration on this plan.
